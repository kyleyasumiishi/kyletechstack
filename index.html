Kyle's Tech Stack

Welcome! This website showcases all of the components that make up one of my favorite places to work, play, and hangout - my personal home office gaming/workstation.

I've loved computers ever since I was a kid. I have a very distinct memory of 14-year-old Kyle randomly picking up a copy of PC World on the way to a family camping trip. It was one of the issues that covered 101 best free software (this one), and I read that issue cover to cover multiple times that summer. In college I became really interested in tinkering with "low-end" computers to sqeeze maximum performance out of relatively inexpensive hardware like Chromebooks and Raspberry Pi, picking up basic skills in Linux and programming along the way.

However despite my passion for tech, I never owned a "high-end" computer until 2020 when I decided to build my own PC. I had a few goals in mind when I finally set out to build it:

  1. Learn how to build a computer. This required dozens of hours of researching parts and learning about how computers work. I enjoy learning new things, and this was definitely a fun - albeit expensive - educational experience.
  2. Build a gaming PC. I wanted to experience my favorite games (Skyrim, Witcher 3, Halo MCC) and new ones with solid hardware on max settings.
  3. Build a powerful productivity workstation. I started building my PC right before COVID-19 took off in the U.S. With remote work looking to become the new norm, I figured 2020 was a good time to splurge on a home workstation setup, including a standing desk, multiple monitors, and an ergonomic chair. I also wanted a powerful PC that would let me pursue my hobby of programming and build cool projects like this website.
